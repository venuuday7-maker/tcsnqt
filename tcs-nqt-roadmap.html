The Core Plan

7-Day Preparation Roadmap

Day 1–2 🧑‍💻 Coding Foundation ~6 hrs/day

DSA Topics to Cover

  • Arrays & Strings (patterns, sliding window)
  • Hashing — frequency maps, two-sum tricks
  • Sorting — merge sort, quick sort logic
  • Binary Search — on arrays + on answer
  • Linked Lists — reverse, detect cycle
  • Recursion — subsets, permutations
  • Stacks & Queues — NGE, balanced brackets

Daily Execution

  • Striver TCS Sheet: solve 40–50 problems across 2 days
  • Use a timer (20 min/problem max)
  • Code in C++/Java/Python — pick ONE language
  • After solving — read optimal solution
  • Revise solved problems before sleeping
  • Focus: correctness first, speed second
⚡ Key Insight: TCS coding questions are medium-level at most. If you can solve 2 questions correctly in the coding round, you are automatically shortlisted for Digital. Solve all 3 optimally → Prime consideration.

Day 3 🧮 Aptitude Blitz ~5 hrs

Quantitative Aptitude

  • Number System — HCF, LCM, remainders
  • Percentages, Profit & Loss
  • Time, Speed & Distance
  • Work & Time problems
  • Probability & Permutation/Combination
  • Data Interpretation (tables, bar graphs)

Reasoning + Verbal

  • Syllogisms & blood relations
  • Coding–Decoding puzzles
  • Series completion & analogies
  • Reading Comprehension — 2 passages
  • Sentence correction & para-jumbles
  • Fill in the blanks (vocabulary)
⚡ Strategy: Attempt all Verbal questions first (fastest points). Skip lengthy DI questions initially. In QA, use elimination — 4 options means 25% chance; narrow to 2 options with logic then guess if needed.
Day 4 💡 CS Fundamentals ~6 hrs

OOP + DBMS

  • 4 Pillars: Encapsulation, Abstraction, Inheritance, Polymorphism
  • Constructor, Destructor, Virtual functions
  • ER Diagrams, Normalization (1NF→BCNF)
  • ACID Properties, Transactions
  • Joins — Inner, Outer, Cross, Self
  • SQL Queries — GROUP BY, HAVING, Subqueries

OS + CN + SDLC

  • Process vs Thread, Deadlock conditions
  • CPU Scheduling: FCFS, SJF, Round Robin
  • Virtual Memory, Paging, Segmentation
  • OSI 7-Layer Model (memorize each layer)
  • TCP vs UDP, HTTP vs HTTPS, DNS
  • Waterfall, Agile, Scrum — SDLC phases
⚡ Interview Hotspots: DBMS Joins + Normalization, OOP Polymorphism examples, Deadlock conditions (Mutual Exclusion, Hold-and-Wait, No Preemption, Circular Wait), OSI layers are asked verbatim.

Day 5 📜 Previous Year Questions ~7 hrs

What to Do

  • Solve 40+ TCS NQT previous year coding Qs
  • Solve 60+ aptitude PYQs (all 3 sections)
  • Note patterns: TCS repeats problems!
  • Maintain an "error log" — wrong answers
  • Revise only weak topics (not everything)

Where to Find PYQs

  • PrepInsta — TCS NQT 2022, 2023, 2024
  • TalentBattle — full sectional tests
  • GeeksforGeeks TCS Prep Page
  • YouTube search: "TCS NQT 2024 coding"
  • Telegram groups: TCS NQT Aspirants
⚡ Pattern Alert: TCS has recycled 30–40% questions from previous years in NQT. Practicing PYQs alone can boost your score by 15–20%. This day is not optional.
Day 6 🎯 Mock Test + Interview Prep ~8 hrs

Mock Test Strategy

  • Attempt ONE full-length TCS NQT mock
  • Strictly follow real exam time limits
  • Analyse every wrong answer after
  • Attempt coding on an actual online compiler
  • Aim: Verbal ≥ 18/24, QA ≥ 18/26, Coding 2/3

Technical Interview Prep

  • "Tell me about yourself" — prepare 90-sec script
  • Explain all 4 OOP concepts with code example
  • Explain your project line-by-line
  • Practise 2 live coding questions on paper
  • Prepare STAR answers for HR questions
⚡ Interview Mindset: TCS interviewers care more about HOW you think than what you know. If you don't know an answer, say "Let me think step by step…" and reason aloud. Never say "I don't know" and stop — always attempt.

Most Asked HR Questions

  • Why TCS?
  • Strength & Weakness
  • Are you willing to relocate?
  • Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
  • What makes you different from others?

Technical Questions Bank

  • Difference: Overloading vs Overriding
  • What is a Deadlock? 4 conditions?
  • 3NF vs BCNF with example
  • Write SELECT with JOIN query
  • What is OSI model Layer 4?
Day 7 ✨ Final Revision + Rest ~4 hrs + Rest

Morning Session (2 hrs)

  • Revise your error log from Days 1–6
  • Glance at ALL coding patterns (not full solutions)
  • Review your "Tell me about yourself" script
  • Read your 1-page CS summary notes

Afternoon Session (2 hrs)

  • Attempt a 1-hour mini aptitude test
  • Revise formula sheet: %, P&L, speed, time
  • Revise SQL query types
  • Pack your documents & ID proof
⚡ The Golden Rule: Stop studying by 6 PM. Have a good meal. Sleep for at least 7–8 hours. A rested brain solves 30% faster. The last-minute panic loop is your biggest enemy — don't fall for it.

What Not To Do

5 Mistakes That Kill Chances

01
Learning new topics instead of mastering exam topics TCS tests a defined set of topics every year. Chasing new DSA concepts 3 days before the exam is the fastest way to score 0 in coding.
02
Skipping Aptitude thinking "I'm a coder" The aptitude cutoff filters out candidates before they even see the coding round. A low aptitude score = no coding round access.
03
Not practising under exam conditions Solving problems without a timer gives a false sense of confidence. TCS has per-section time limits. Practice with strict timers from Day 3.
04
Ignoring the interview round Students clear NQT and then fumble basic OOP or SQL in the interview. Tech + HR prep together = complete offer letter.
05
Burning out the night before the exam An exhausted brain loses 30% cognitive speed. The last-minute cramming panic is real — and almost always hurts more than it helps.
